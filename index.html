Why we exist

Culture is our
way of giving.

FYLH San Diego is a youth-led nonprofit working to fund hospitals that serve underprivileged communities, by sharing the Indian cultural heritage we grew up with right here at home.

"Quality healthcare, in a not-for-profit mode, for a community that has gone without it for far too long."

The 200-bed multi-specialty Sanjivani Hospital was built in an under-served part of rural Andhra Pradesh, India, a region with high rates of malaria, typhoid, diabetes, cardiac disease and infant mortality, yet little access to well-equipped care. Every workshop we teach, every basket we sell, and every camp we run helps support care for the families who rely on it.

What we do

Young people, real work,
a cause that matters.

Our youth leaders don't just raise money. They teach, cook, plant, perform and serve, building real skills while doing real good across San Diego.

Cultural workshops

Ganesh idol-making, Diwali diya painting, Sankranti kites, rangoli and folk art, bringing Indian traditions to San Diego families.

Summer camps

Two-week camps where youth leaders teach what they love: coding, science-fair mentorship, art, tennis and dance.

Community service

Cooking and serving at Father Joe's Villages, tidying the decks of the USS Midway, and tending the EarthLab garden.

Fundraisers

Food carnivals, bake sales, saplings, rakhis and handmade festival baskets. Every dollar goes toward care.

The art we share

Folk traditions, taught by hand.

The art forms our youth leaders teach at camps and workshops, each one a piece of living Indian heritage.
